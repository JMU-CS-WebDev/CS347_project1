Facts

Colors

The colors of Alpha Omega Epsilon are royal blue, silver, and white. Each of these colors are symbolic to the sorority and their meanings are known only to the Sisters of Alpha Omega Epsilon.

Flowers

The flowers of Alpha Omega Epsilon Sorority are the white carnation for the candidates, the white rose for the actives, and the calla lily for the alumna.

Mascot

A Mascot is any person, animal or thing thought to bring good luck by being present. Therefore, the Gamma Chapter proposed that a mascot be chosen to represent our sorority. After discussion it was decided that the dolphin be chosen as our mascot.

We feel that a dolphin would be an excellent representation of AΩE for the following reasons:

  • Dolphins are considered to be one of the most intellectual mammals known to man, just as STEM fields are considered an extremely intellectual career.
  • Happy and sociable are two common descriptions of a dolphinâ€™s behavior. As the dolphins display these characteristics so do the sisters of AΩE.
  • There are forty different species of dolphins, which coexist in friendship. Women of different backgrounds come together with AΩE and work in friendship.
  • Dolphins are helpful with navigation and rescue efforts. The sisters of our sorority are helpful through community service and support systems for fellow female STEM students.
  • Female dolphins are livelier and more inquisitive than male dolphins. The sisters of AΩE are known for their inquisitive minds.
  • Dolphins communicate through a distinctive pattern of clicks and whistling sounds of their echolocations. The Chapters of AΩE communicate through an integrated network.
  • For centuries, sailors have regarded the presence of dolphins near ships as a sign of a smooth voyage and good luck. A mascot is a symbol of good luck and we feel that the dolphin would be an ideal mascot for AΩE.

The meaning and symbolism for the crest is explained during the active initiation ceremony. The meaning and symbolism are known only to the members.
